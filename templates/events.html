Vastra Vismaya

Step into the vibrant world of traditional Indian textiles at VastraVismay, a celebration of regional sarees that narrate stories of heritage, craftsmanship, and elegance. From the royal Kanjeevaram silks of Tamil Nadu, the earthy Chanderi and Maheshwari of Madhya Pradesh, to the intricate Jamdani from Bengal, and the colorful Bandhani of Gujarat and Rajasthan—witness the artistry that transforms threads into timeless drapes. Meet the local weavers and textile artists who’ve preserved these legacies for generations. Experience live demonstrations of loom work, natural dyeing, block printing, and much more—all under one roof.

Venu:Render Road, Textile Market, Surat, Gujarat

Event Timing: 18th Aug 2025 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM

Past Events

Bhitti Chitra

Feburary 2025 • Nirona Village, Kutch, Gujarat

Bhitti-Chitra was a curated exhibition that paid homage to India’s ancient and evolving tradition of wall painting — not as mere decoration, but as a living language of people, rituals, and places. Spanning from the tribal patterns of the Warlis to the divine symbolism of Pattachitra, from the vivid red ochres of Sohrai to the geometric expressions of Gond and Bhil art,this event offered a multi-sensory walk through the nation’s mural heritage. The exhibition space transformed into a storytelling gallery — where hand-painted panels, archival prints, and life-sized replicas of rural walls recreated the intimacy of homes and temples. Each art form was accompanied by curated narratives, videos, and audio snippets explaining the cultural significance and oral traditions that accompany them. Highlights of the event included a live mural session where guests could witness artists recreate sections of village walls using natural dyes and handmade brushes, an artist talk series titled “Echoes from the Wall”, and interactive corners where attendees could try their hands at making miniature mural swatches under artisan guidance. Bhitti-Chitra was not just a visual experience — it was a heartfelt tribute to the hands that turn bare mud walls into canvases of collective memory, resistance, worship, and joy.

Taal-Tarang : Rhythms of the soil

October 2024 • Bhubaneswar, Odisha

Set under a starlit sky and lit by lanterns, “Taal-Tarang” was a soundscape carved out of soil, wood, and wind. The event brought together folk musicians from diverse geographies — the vibrant beats of the Dholak, the soul-tugging tunes of the Algoza, and the haunting call of the Been. Every performance was an unfolding of a region’s story, history, and ritual. The concept was simple: a round mud amphitheatre, no formal stage, and no sound systems — only the raw power of instruments and untrained but divine voices. Visitors sat cross-legged, sipped masala chai, and witnessed performances that ranged from love ballads of Rajasthan to tribal battle songs of Jharkhand. The heart of the event was “Raag-Saanjh,” a collaborative evening jam where different musicians blended their instruments in unrehearsed harmony. Some guests even danced barefoot around the fire, some simply closed their eyes and let the vibrations sink in. It was not a concert — it was a prayer in rhythm. “Taal-Tarang” reminded us that before Spotify, before records, and before studios — music was born in hearts, heard in forests, and carried in feet.
